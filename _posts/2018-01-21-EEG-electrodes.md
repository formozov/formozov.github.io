### EEG electrodes

While reading on the electric and magnetic fields produced by the brain and the related problems of reading such a signals
I stared to think about possible-hypothetical (quasi)-invasive methods that could actually increase the conductivity of the tissue 
that separates the macroscopic areas that generate electric fields and electrodes of EEG.

To achieve the brain one need to pass through scalp, skull and cerebral spinal fluid (CSF).
The problem of conductivity may appear at the very begging at the attachment of the electrode to the skin.
One may think about increasing conductivity of the  skin by implanting the highly conductive materials into the skin: 
simply speaking “making a tattoo” with nano-particles of gold or silver.

I started to search and found the article that  there is a [group](http://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021%2Facsnano.7b02182) that actually performed
temporary tattoo for electrophysiological
purposes with graphene.  Another [group from Tel Aviv](https://www.nature.com/articles/srep25727) demonstrated usefulness of temporary tatoo in electromyography.
They found that the contact of electrode with a skin is quite good. [Someya group](http://www.ntech.t.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/) from Tokyo provided similar research [https://www.nature.com/articles/nnano.2017.125](https://www.nature.com/articles/nnano.2017.125).
Specialising in organic transistors they go even further attaching different electronic components on the skin including visual indicators.

So far I didn’t check yet the estimation for the relative contributions into the total conductivity from the different aforementioned layers,
but intuitively it seems that the major contribution must be skull’s tissue. At least for a forehead there is a good example of endoscopic 
[osteoma removal in plastic surgery](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tuRrxI_awlE). So, in principle, the surface of the skull must be easily accessible and 
contacts could be imported on the skull surface. Recently it was anounced about development of the [minimally invasive device for tracking of the number
of epilepsy events](https://www.wysscenter.ch/project/epilepsy-monitoring-seizure-forecasts/).

To go deeper into the skull is quite problematic. The possible way could be development of the method of doping the skull’s tissue with 
conductive materials. Though it is probably not a very nice idea so far.
